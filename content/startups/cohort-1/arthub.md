ArtHub is a community-oriented platform purpose-built for artists to share work directly with other artists, galleries, schools, and employers — with built-in protections against AI data scraping. What started as a simple art-sharing app evolved into a community project-display platform.

Professors at the University of the Arts London (the world's largest arts university) have asked to use it in their classrooms. Founded by Ian Baime (CMC '24, Philosophy/Data Science), who conceived the idea after a gap semester at UAL where he discovered there were no good tools for artist visibility.
